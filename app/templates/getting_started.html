Getting Started

Basic Ingredients

Beer can be made from just about anything! But the 4 main ingredients are:

  • water
  • barley
  • hops
  • yeast

Thats it?? Then how come there are so many different kinds of beer out there? How can you possibly have such variance from the same 4 basic ingredients? Read On!

Recipe

Ok, so how much of each ingredient do you need? That depends on the recipe, but here's a simple (and crude) recipe.

Ingredient Amount % Composition
Water 7 gal (~ 60 lbs) 85%
Barley 10 lbs 15%
Hops 5 oz < 1 %
Yeast billions of cells < 1%

Basic Steps

  1. Extract sugars from the grain (mashing)

    Equipment You'll need:

    • mash tun (holds the grain while you extract the sugars/ serves as a filter to remove the sugars from the husks)
    • thermometer

      • Boil the wort (sugary water) and add hops

        Equipment You'll need:

        • brew kettle (about 10 gal capacity)
        • propane stove

      • Cool the hopped wort and pitch the yeast

        Equipment You'll need:

        • wort chiller
        • fine-mesh seive/strainer
        • hydrometer (for measuring specific gravity)
        • carboys with airlocks (fermentation containers)
        • sanitizer (iodophor)

      • Wait (~ 2 weeks) for the yeast to do thie work (fermenting) then bottle/keg the beer

        Equipment You'll need:

      • racking cane and tubing (we'll need to siphon the beer)
      • bottles/caps or kegs
      • sanitizer (iodophor)
